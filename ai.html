🌍 My Perspective on AI

AI isn't just a tech buzzword — for me, it's a partner in thinking. I see AI as an amplifier for human creativity, speed, and learning. It’s not here to replace curiosity, but to **extend it**.

I'm not here just to “use” AI. I’m here to **understand** it — how it works, how it reasons, and how it changes the way we learn and solve problems.

🧠 How I Use AI

I use AI almost daily. Here's what I'm doing with it so far:

  • Bug Bounty: I ask AI to explain HTTP requests, payloads, cookie/session behavior, and how JSON APIs work
  • Linux Learning: I use AI to decode terminal tools, permissions, Nmap scans, and hardening methods
  • Computer Networking: I ask about OSI layers, scanning techniques, proxy chains, DNS, and even packet behavior
  • English Speaking: I talk to AI to practice fluency, get feedback, and build sentence confidence
  • Project Building: I use AI to generate starter code, structure my notes, and even help build this website!

🛠️ Tools & Concepts I Explored

  • ChatGPT: for breaking down complex topics and helping with real-time ideas
  • Sora (video AI): I discovered how video generation tools can be used for storytelling, education, or simulation
  • AI in Cybersecurity: I’ve learned how AI is used in malware detection, phishing prevention, and even automated recon
  • Prompt Engineering: I’m learning how to talk to AI better by structuring smarter questions and giving context

🚀 Why I Think AI Matters in the Future

AI will be part of everything — whether you’re hacking, teaching, coding, or building products. But here’s the difference:

> Those who understand how AI works will shape it. > Those who don’t will just consume it.

I want to be someone who **shapes it**, or at least **guides it with purpose**.

  • Use AI to automate repetitive cybersecurity tasks
